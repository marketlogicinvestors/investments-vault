The Thesis in One Paragraph

What you’d say if the partner stopped you in the hallway.

Intellectible is a vertical AI platform that automates the full revenue capture cycle — bid identification, RFP parsing, proposal generation, and margin-protected pricing — for enterprise service providers selling into U.S. government and large institutional buyers.

The company has built a proprietary workflow runtime on Kubernetes that executes each customer’s SOPs as containerized, auditable workflows, fundamentally different from a prompt wrapper or a horizontal AI tool. ARR grew from ~$100K to ~$760K in roughly 14 months on $1.7M of pre-Seed capital, with the Pricing Engine — the highest-ACV upsell and the core of the expansion thesis — now in production at multiple named accounts. The priced $2.95M Seed round at a $12M pre-money closed led by Bread and Butter Ventures. The remaining SAFE capacity at a $20M cap is the entry point, and the three-week SPV timeline and $250K remaining allocation make this a now-or-miss decision. The primary risk is speed of category capture before AI add-on tools from Deltek, GovDash, or Microsoft Copilot compress the window; the Pricing Engine’s production status and HHS’s trajectory to a $400K logo together represent the most important de-risking developments since the prior pass.

What We Like
  • Revenue operations automation for GovCon is structurally manual, under-served by both legacy ERP and generic AI, and high-stakes enough that buyers pay for verified accuracy.
  • Pricing Engine in production at named accounts (Team OTG full deployment, Apex Site Solutions light, HHS EDU/Resorts/Government in active signing) de-risks the single largest prior concern.
  • 85%+ gross margins with GPU/cloud cost below $4K per $100K ARR, trending to $2K — software-grade unit economics, not services.
  • CEO is a repeat founder (Pi-Top: $0 → $10M ARR in under 3 years on $35M raised, PE exit). CTO contributed to Apple Vision Pro; CPO led multi-million-dollar Salesforce implementations.
  • 0% reported churn within ICP; HHS land-and-expand is hard evidence of $24K → $400K+ account trajectory.
  • QSBS-qualified, $0 outstanding debt, 12% option pool in place — clean structure.
What Concerns Us
  • SAFE converts above the priced round ($20M cap vs. $15M post-money) — SAFE participants accept a slightly worse entry price than Seed investors.
  • HHS concentration: trajectory to $400K logo would lift one customer past 50% of contracted ARR before new-logo dilution reduces it.
  • Cohort is small (15+ logos, ~24 months) — 0% ICP churn is a positive signal, not yet a retention conclusion.
  • Founder-led sales through ~$3M ARR is a deliberate sequencing decision, but it is a real single-point dependency until the two AEs and implementation specialists ramp in July–August 2026.
  • Model ARR ($553K booked, non-service run-rate per the projection model tab) is slightly below the memo-stated $760K contracted ARR — align on the correct current figure with the company before wiring.

§ 04

The Competitive Advantage

Why this platform is structurally hard to replicate.

Intellectible’s defensibility sits below the AI engines at the runtime and data layer.

  1. Production Workflow Runtime, not a Prompt Wrapper. Fully containerized units on managed Kubernetes, strong multi-tenant isolation, distributed orchestration with specialized microservices. ~2.5 years of build, fundamentally different from in-process graph orchestration (LangGraph, LlamaIndex) or what Salesforce Flow can express without hitting platform limits.
  2. SOP Digital Twin and Compounding Switching Costs. Within 1–2 weeks the platform encodes right-fit criteria, go/no-go gates, required artifacts, and routing/approval logic, embedded in inbox, CRM, and work tools. Within 30–90 days rule sets tighten, edge cases are captured, and pricing governance is institutionalized. Rip-and-replace becomes operationally and politically expensive.
  3. AI-Governed Pricing, not AI-Generated Pricing. Customer-specific rate tables, formulas, assumptions, and approval paths encoded as workflow logic. AI extracts and normalizes pricing-relevant data but does not output a final price without deterministic rails and human-in-the-loop validation. Reported effective accuracy on fields used for pricing: ~100% verified.
  4. High-Fidelity Document and Vision Extraction. Multi-pass extraction, reconciliation, normalization into pricing and compliance schemas with provenance, and deterministic validation. Custom-engineered IP layered on commodity OCR and cloud primitives — the defensible value is in the validation stack.
  5. Embedded Distribution via Integrations. OAuth connectors into Salesforce, Zoho, Microsoft, Google, Slack, HubSpot, Monday, plus webhooks, APIs, and MCP support. The product becomes the automation layer running revenue ops, not a sidecar UI.

Where Intellectible is vulnerable

  • Incumbents (Deltek, GovDash) have stronger brand and broader distribution in GovCon.
  • Salesforce and Microsoft Copilot could ship add-on features that compress pricing on the entry GovCon Engine.
  • FedRAMP-baseline accounts (defense primes, sensitive federal IT) are not in ICP today.

§ 08

Market Context

TAM, tailwinds, and structural opportunity.

Market framing: roughly 8,700 U.S. enterprise service providers generate >$100M in annual revenue; ~188,000 generate >$10M. Each runs company-specific revenue processes — too bespoke for generic SaaS, too important to leave manual. Intellectible replaces per-company bespoke work with a scalable platform.

GovCon sub-market: HHS alone spends an estimated $100M–$125M annually on software at its ~$2B revenue scale — Intellectible could realistically capture 1–2% of that with full platform penetration.

Structural Tailwinds

  • AI is collapsing the historical tradeoff between bespoke enterprise software and vertical SaaS.
  • Government procurement complexity (RFP volume, compliance, re-competes) is increasing.
  • Service providers are under margin pressure — bid automation with defensible pricing directly protects margin.
  • Incumbent GovCon software (Deltek, Unanet) is legacy-monolith architecture; organic AI response is 2–4 years away or an acquisition.
Comparable category outcomes: Harvey (legal), Abridge (clinical documentation), and Vanta (compliance) are recent vertical AI companies producing category-defining outcomes in legacy, document-heavy markets. Intellectible is the analogous platform for revenue operations in enterprise services.
