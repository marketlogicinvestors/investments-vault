§ 01

The thesis in one paragraph

What you’d say if the partner stopped you in the hallway.

Intellectible is an AI-native enterprise learning platform that converts unstructured institutional knowledge— policy documents, process wikis, veteran employee expertise—into structured, adaptive micro-credential pathways with built-in assessment and mastery verification. It competes in the $280B corporate learning market against Workday Learning, Cornerstone, and LinkedIn Learning, not by matching feature depth but by collapsing the content-creation cycle from months to hours. The wedge is speed: an L&D team can ingest a 400-page operations manual and generate a certified, auto-assessed learning path in under 90 minutes. The risk is the same as every AI productivity wedge in 2026: LLM commoditization is six months away from giving every competitor the same capability natively. The question is whether Intellectible’s 340-customer enterprise footprint, proprietary knowledge-graph layer, and LMS integrations are defensible enough to survive the model-parity window.

The 89% knowledge retention lift is the deck’s most powerful claim and its most unverified one. If it survives third-party audit, this company is worth a $10–18M Series A pre-money as a standalone platform play. If it does not, the moat narrows to LMS network effects and integration depth, which exist but are not alone sufficient at a premium multiple.

WHAT WE LIKE

AI-native content creation with measurable learning outcomes in a market where incumbents are workflow-heavy and AI-light. 340+ enterprise customers on $6.2M raised implies capital-efficient early traction. Natural acquisition target for Workday, SAP, or Salesforce.

WHAT CONCERNS US

LLM commoditization threatens the content-generation moat within 12–24 months. Customer concentration unknown. Retention and expansion revenue undisclosed. Outcomes data entirely self-reported.

RECOMMENDED NEXT STEP

Issue data-room request: ARR, NRR, logo concentration, outcomes methodology, LLM cost structure, cap table. Decision deadline: T+10 business days.

Intellectible’s core product is an AI content engine that ingests unstructured enterprise knowledge—PDFs, Confluence wikis, Notion databases, recorded training sessions, Slack threads— and generates structured micro-credential pathways with auto-generated assessments, knowledge checks, and mastery gates. The output is portable: learning paths export natively to Workday Learning, Cornerstone, SAP SuccessFactors, and SCORM-compliant LMS environments.

The differentiation is in the knowledge graph layer underneath the generation pipeline. Rather than treating each document as a standalone context window, Intellectible maintains a per-customer knowledge graph that maps concepts, dependencies, and mastery prerequisites across the full corpus. This allows the system to detect knowledge gaps at the cohort level (“28% of your CS team cannot correctly apply the escalation policy”) and to surface just-in-time remediation paths without requiring an L&D team to manually redesign curriculum.

Point 1: the 89% retention claim

What it says, what it requires, where it could break.

The deck’s headline outcome is an 89% improvement in knowledge retention measured at 30 days post-training, versus a 34% baseline for instructor-led corporate training [DECK]. This is a 2.6x lift on a metric that, if independently validated, would be the strongest single argument for the product in any competitive evaluation.

The retention claim requires scrutiny. Corporate learning “retention” has no universal standard definition. Is this measured by re-assessment score, self-report, or behavioral observation? What is the denominator (all enrolled learners, or completers only)? What is the attrition-to-completion rate? Diligence ask C1 covers the full methodology.

The content-creation time claim (under 90 minutes for a 400-page document) is, paradoxically, more verifiable and more consequential to the near-term commercial motion. This is what L&D buyers will test in a proof-of-concept. If it holds, the sales cycle shortens dramatically because the buyer can experience ROI in the first demo session.

§ 04

The thing that’s actually defensible

Why the knowledge graph layer matters more than the generation model.

LLM-based content generation is commoditizing. GPT-4o, Claude 3.5, and Gemini 1.5 Pro can all convert a PDF into quiz questions with reasonable quality today. Workday and Cornerstone will have native AI content generation features within 12–18 months. The generation layer is not defensible.

The knowledge graph is the part that could be. A per-tenant graph that has been trained on 18 months of customer knowledge, mapped to their org structure, their role taxonomy, and their specific compliance requirements, with linked assessment data showing which concepts individual employees have mastered and which they have not—that is not easily replaced by a native LMS feature. It is also not easily migrated. This is the switching-cost thesis.

WHAT THE GRAPH LAYER CONTAINS

  • Concept ontology mapped from full customer document corpus
  • Prerequisite dependency chains (must master A before B)
  • Per-employee mastery state at concept level
  • Assessment item bank with difficulty calibration per concept
  • Cohort-level gap analytics (which concepts is the team failing)

WHAT IT COULD BECOME

  • Compliance audit trail: prove specific employees mastered specific policies
  • Predictive risk: flag employees with mastery gaps on safety-critical content
  • M&A integration: onboarding new workforce to acquiring company’s knowledge corpus
  • Workforce intelligence: which roles carry the most institutional knowledge concentration risk
§ 05

Point 2: the enterprise GTM motion

340 customers on $6.2M raised. The math that matters.

Intellectible reports 340+ enterprise customers [DECK] on $6.2M total capital raised [EXT: PITCHBOOK]. At 28 employees, this implies a revenue-per-employee ratio in the range of $39K–$60K ARR/employee—consistent with an early-stage SaaS company that has not yet scaled its GTM function. The capital efficiency is notable: 340 customers on $6.2M total raised is achievable only via PLG, inbound, or extremely tight sales cycles.

The commercial risk is customer concentration. Without disclosed logo-level revenue breakdowns, we cannot assess whether 340 customers represents $1.1M ARR distributed across 340 $3K–$5K SMB accounts, or 20 enterprise accounts driving 80% of revenue with 320 long-tail pilots. These are fundamentally different businesses with different Series A underwriting logic.

BULL: DISTRIBUTED BASE

340 customers with average ACV of $3K–$6K across multiple verticals implies a product-led growth motion with low CAC and strong early network effects. Upgradeable as the knowledge graph deepens per tenant.

BEAR: CONCENTRATED PILOTS

20 enterprise logos at $40K+ ACV with 320 unpaid or sub-$1K pilot accounts creates dangerous revenue concentration at Series A. One churn event materially impairs the growth narrative.

§ 08

TAM, tailwinds, friction

$280B+ corporate learning market with AI-native transformation underway.

TOTAL ADDRESSABLE [EXT: PITCHBOOK]

$280B+ global corporate learning market. AI-native content tools sub-segment growing at 18–24% CAGR. Compliance training, onboarding, and skills development are the highest-volume use cases and highest-willingness-to-pay segments.

TAILWINDS

  • AI productivity wave driving L&D budget expansion (more content needed, faster)
  • Remote-first workforce requires scalable async learning
  • Skills-based hiring creating demand for verifiable micro-credentials
  • Compliance complexity increasing (AI governance, data privacy) requiring continuous upskilling
  • M&A integration wave driving demand for rapid knowledge transfer tools

HEADWINDS

  • LLM commoditization: every LMS vendor will have native AI content generation by 2027
  • L&D budget is discretionary and first cut in downturns
  • Enterprise sales cycles: 6–12 months for large logo acquisition
  • SCORM and xAPI lock-in creates switching cost in both directions (helps and hurts)
  • Outcomes data quality: “learning ROI” is notoriously hard to prove to CFOs

Decision and conditions

RECOMMENDATION

Track · Conditional Pass · Re-evaluate Post-Data-Room

Intellectible warrants further diligence, not yet a term sheet. The market size, team pedigree, and capital efficiency are real and above-average for this stage. The LLM commoditization risk and unverified outcomes data are genuine. We recommend transmitting the formal Information Request (Ref: ML-2026-IT-DR-01) immediately and re-presenting to IC within 21 days if responses clear the P0 thresholds.

CONDITIONS TO ADVANCE
  • Verified TTM ARR ≥ $1M growing > 8% MoM
  • NRR ≥ 110% (net revenue expansion within accounts)
  • No single customer > 20% of ARR
  • Outcomes methodology survives third-party review or customer reference validation
  • AI/LLM COGS ≤ 15% of revenue (gross margin defensible at scale)
  • CTO equity vesting and retention plan confirmed before term sheet
CONDITIONS TO PASS
  • NRR below 100% in trailing 12 months
  • ARR below $700K or flat / declining MoM growth
  • Single customer > 35% of ARR
  • No defensible answer on LLM commoditization (product roadmap)
  • AI/LLM inference cost > 30% of revenue (margin structure broken at scale)
  • Undisclosed prior failed Series A process within last 6 months

Proposed indicative terms if conditions are met: $0.5–1.5M check size into Series A at $12–18M pre-money, with information rights and pro-rata. Lead position not pursued at this check size; participation rights under a B2B SaaS specialist lead preferred.
