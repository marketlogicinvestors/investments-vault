§ 01

Executive Summary

The thesis in one paragraph

Intellectible is a government-focused SaaS company building AI-powered proposal and pricing automation for federal contractors. The company has strong early product-market fit with the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and adjacent agencies, real revenue (~$760K ARR), and a fundamentally sound technical wedge in the Pricing Engine. However, this is not a Series A opportunity. It is a concentrated customer-risk scenario with >50% ARR from a single anchor account, zero disclosed customer churn methodology, unverified GPU cost compression assumptions, and a $250K+ ARR discrepancy between reported figures and the financial model. The recommended action is Track · Conditional Pass with a 12-month monitoring deadline: advance criteria require HHS concentration to fall below 40%, Pricing Engine adoption to reach 8+ production deployments with publicly referenceable metrics, and net retention to exceed 90% with 18+ month cohort tenure.

What we like

First-mover positioning in AI-powered federal proposal automation. The Pricing Engine represents a category-defining wedge if it can scale before incumbents (Salesforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft) ship overlapping features. The HHS anchor account validates product-market fit in a high-value vertical.

What concerns us

HHS concentration risk is the loudest signal. The lack of transparent churn cohort data, unverified GPU cost compression, ARR variance, and missing HHS contract termination clause disclosure creates an information asymmetry that prevents confident underwriting. The company's claims lack independent customer verification.

Recommended next step

Track with Q2 2027 monitoring deadline. Advance requires: (1) HHS dilution below 40% via 4+ new $100K+ logos, (2) Pricing Engine reaches 8+ production instances with customer-verified ROI, (3) net retention >90% with 18+ month cohort data. Pass if HHS contracts delay/decline or ARR growth <3% MoM for two consecutive quarters.

§ 02

The Binary Variables

What this deal actually turns on

This is not a vote on whether Intellectible has product-market fit. It is a vote on two variables:

  1. Can the Pricing Engine become a category-defining wedge before point-tool incumbents (Salesforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft) ship overlapping features?
  2. Can HHS expansion economics replicate across 5+ comparable logos before concentration becomes a Series A liability?

If both answer "yes" by Q2 2027, this becomes a fundable Series A at $15-25M pre. If either answers "no," this becomes an M&A conversation with Deltek, Salesforce Gov Cloud, or a federal systems integrator.

§ 03

Missing Diligence

The hidden data gaps

  • HHS Contract Structure: No disclosure of termination clauses or volume commitments for the anchor account (>50% of ARR). Multi-year lock or annual renewal?
  • Churn Reconciliation: "0% actual churn within ICP" lacks cohort-level breakdown. The model includes 25% annual churn, creating an un-reconciled 25-point gap.
  • GPU Cost Compression: Projected reduction from $4K to $2K per $100K ARR lacks vendor commitments or technical validation.
  • ARR Discrepancy: $250K+ variance between $760K reported ARR and $509,782 in the financial model. Which number is auditable?

§ 05

Competitive Reality

The incumbent migration risk

While the deck highlights workflow runtime as a differentiator, incumbents like Deltek ProPricer present significant migration risks. The company's "loudest signal" is the lack of public ROI validation from customers. Metrics regarding the HHS EDU rollout are company-reported, not customer-verified.

The market window for the Pricing Engine wedge is estimated at 12-18 months before Salesforce Gov Cloud or ServiceNow integrate comparable AI pricing modules into existing federal contractor workflows.

§ 07

The HHS Paradox

Strongest proof point, greatest risk

HHS is the company's strongest validation but also its concentration risk. The mitigation strategy requires adding 4 new logos per month, a significant ramp from the current base of 15 paying customers.

If the HHS EDU expansion trajectory to $400K continues as projected (currently >50% of ARR), concentration will worsen before it improves. The risk is binary: HHS contract renewal success vs. termination/renegotiation.

§ 08

Advance & Pass Criteria

12-month monitoring gates

Advance Criteria (12-month Track)

  • HHS concentration dilutes below 40% of ARR through 4+ new $100K+ logos
  • Pricing Engine adoption reaches 8+ production deployments with publicly referenceable metrics
  • Net retention stabilizes above 90% with cohort data demonstrating 18+ month tenure

Pass Criteria

  • HHS contracts down or delays Pricing Engine rollout
  • ARR growth rate falls below 3% month-over-month for 2 consecutive quarters
  • Pricing Engine deployment stalls at <5 production instances by Q4 2026

Monitoring Deadline: May 2027 Series A announcement or next fundraise, whichever occurs first.

§ 09

Conclusion

Fundable, not exceptional

The deal is fundable but not exceptional. With $1.2M cash projected for the Series A (early 2028), the company faces high macro sensitivity. The Pricing Engine wedge has real category-capture potential, but customer concentration and missing diligence create downside asymmetry.

The committee is advised to Track until Q2 2027 to see if:

  1. The Pricing Engine proves category-defining traction (8+ referenceable deployments)
  2. HHS concentration dilutes to <40% via new logo acquisition
  3. Churn, GPU costs, and ARR variance are reconciled with third-party audit

If all three resolve positively, re-evaluate at Series A pricing. If any fail, this becomes an M&A target for Deltek or federal systems integrators, not a venture-scale opportunity.
