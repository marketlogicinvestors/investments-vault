§ 05Commercial & Channel Footprint

Point 2: What the "Nordstrom channel" actually is

Drop-ship marketplace economics ≠ wholesale relationship economics.

The deck refers to "an established and growing retail channel with Nordstrom." External review of Nordstrom's emerging-brand model Ext: Perplexity/Nordstrom Supplier Program indicates Nordstrom engages smaller brands through a drop-ship / marketplace model, not seasonal wholesale POs. In this model: the brand ships direct to the customer; Nordstrom takes a commission (rather than purchasing inventory upfront); payouts are weekly or regular; floor space and inventory commitment are not part of the deal. This is materially different from a traditional wholesale channel.

This is not necessarily bad — drop-ship reduces working-capital exposure and accelerates listing. But it changes how revenue and margin should be modeled, and it changes what "an established retail channel" means as a signal of velocity. Without sell-through data, we cannot tell whether Nordstrom is a contributing revenue channel or a discovery-only listing.

Discrepancy 5 · Nordstrom Channel Characterization

Deck claim: "Established and growing retail channel with Nordstrom"

External finding: Nordstrom emerging-brand engagements typically use drop-ship/marketplace model Ext. Traditional wholesale (seasonal POs, floor space) requires proven velocity at scale Dapper Boi has not externally demonstrated.

Implication: Revenue from this channel is likely commission-based marketplace revenue, not wholesale POs. Margin profile, working-capital intensity, and revenue durability differ materially.

Resolution required: Specify channel type (drop-ship vs. wholesale POs), revenue contribution 2024, sell-through metrics, payout terms.
