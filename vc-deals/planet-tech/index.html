§ 01

Thesis in One Paragraph

What you'd say if the partner stopped you in the hallway.

PLANET.TECH is an angel-round bet on the only cattle health collar that measures pulse oximetry through hide — a sensor no competitor offers — at the $35 COGS and $6M post-money cap, distributed through veterinarians into a $20B preventable-loss market at the center of the worst cattle health crisis in 60 years, with a climate second act that could convert the same platform into one of the largest clean-fuel infrastructure assets on the planet.

The entry logic is straightforward: Halter just raised a $220M Series E at a $2B valuation to put GPS collars on cows. PLANET.TECH enters the same category with deeper biometrics (pulse ox + GPS + dual accelerometer + on-device AI), lower COGS ($35 vs. $120–175 competitor ASP), a differentiated distribution model (vet practices as warm-intro channels to 50+ farms), and a climate-revenue second act Halter does not have — all at a $6M post-money cap, which is 333× cheaper than the Halter benchmark. The risk is not whether the category is real. The risk is whether the pulse-ox-through-hide claim holds in real field conditions. The entire investment thesis depends on that single technical question, and the Texas A&M pilot in September 2026 is the most important catalyst in the deal.

The committee's vote is not "Is PLANET.TECH a good company?" The vote is: Is the sensor claim credible enough to warrant a small, option-sized position before the Texas A&M pilot validates or invalidates it?
↑ What Compels the Investment
  • NWS outbreak is real and government-confirmed — closest to US border in 60 years. Demand for early cattle-disease detection has never been higher.
  • Pulse oximetry categorical gap is independently corroborated. No current market product offers transcutaneous blood oxygen on a cattle collar.
  • Halter's $220M Series E at $2B proves category, proves investor demand, and provides an exit ceiling. Entry at $6M = 333× discount to validated comparable.
  • $35 COGS, if verified, destroys the pricing moat of every incumbent and enables aggressive market capture before they can respond.
  • Vet-channel GTM has structural leverage that no current competitor uses — routes around the high-CAC direct-farm-sales trap that kills ag-tech hardware.
  • Dual revenue streams: one hardware acquisition, two subscription layers (Mootality + Moola). Compounding data moat as herd population grows.
↓ What Kills the Investment
  • Pulse-ox signal is noisy through bovine hide in field conditions. False positive rate is too high for vet/farmer trust. Core differentiation collapses.
  • 17K signed up / 46 farms is a waitlist, not backlog. When devices ship, conversion to paying customers is weak. ARR ramp stalls.
  • $35 COGS is component-only. Fully-loaded hardware cost at volume is $65–90, making $120/head/year economics uncompetitive vs. alternatives.
  • Halter announces biometric sensors on its next platform (12–24 months). $220M capital closes PLANET.TECH's window before distribution is locked.
  • 8-person equity-only team is key-person fragile. Post-angel round burn jumps to market-rate. $500K is consumed before pre-seed opens.
  • Moola methane capture remains commercially unproven. Climate second act distracts team and capital from the harder health monitoring business.

§ 03

The Pulse Oximetry Wedge

From sensor claim to clinical outcome — the single most important question in this deal.

The investment thesis rests on one technical claim: that PLANET.TECH's collar can read heart rate and blood oxygen through bovine hide reliably enough to enable disease detection in advance of visual symptoms. This claim is independently corroborated as categorically unique — no current cattle monitoring platform (Merck/Allflex, CowManager, smaXtec, Halter) offers transcutaneous SpO₂ measurement.[3][5] The gap is real. What has not been validated is whether the signal is clinically useful at the accuracy and reliability required for commercial disease-state detection.

Critical · Diligence Gate 1
CRITICAL
Signal Accuracy vs. Reference Standard

What is the measured accuracy for heart rate and SpO₂ vs. a veterinary-grade reference device across multiple breeds, hide colors, hair lengths, and movement states?

Critical · Field Robustness
CRITICAL
Environmental Signal Degradation

How does signal quality degrade under mud, rain, cold, heat, collar fit variation, sweating, and running? What is the signal loss rate in real Texas / California ranching conditions?

High · Clinical Utility
HIGH
Sensitivity / Specificity by Condition

What is detection sensitivity and specificity for each of the 17 targeted conditions? False positive rate above ~15% will undermine vet and farmer trust and kill retention.

High · Value Proposition
HIGH
Lead Time vs. Visual Inspection

What is the measured early-detection lead time compared to visual inspection and activity-only systems? The commercial ROI bridge depends entirely on this number.

Medium · Validation Catalyst
MEDIUM
Texas A&M Pilot Protocol

What are the specific endpoints, control group structure, and statistical power of the Sept 2026 100-cow P1 pilot? This is the first structured validation event — request the protocol now.

Medium · IP Defense
MEDIUM
Patent Claim Scope

What is the specific claim language in the 7 filed patents? Narrow claims limited to a single LED wavelength or single breed would provide limited defensibility vs. a well-funded competitor's design-around.

The deck's only sensor data point is "BPM 86 → 112, Day 01, Test 01." That is an anecdote, not a study. The fact that the Texas A&M pilot is framed as a September 2026 milestone — after the angel close and first 1,000 devices ship — means investors are placing capital before the pivotal validation event. This is appropriate for an angel-stage pre-seed: the round is explicitly designed to generate this proof, not to rely on it. The committee's job is to assess whether the team and technical architecture are credible enough to warrant a small option-sized position before that proof is in hand.

§ 10

The Moola / Methane Option

Extraordinary upside framed as a free call option — do not let it carry or kill the base case.

FAO estimates livestock supply chains account for approximately 12–14.5% of global anthropogenic GHG emissions, with cattle responsible for ~62% of that.[10][11] PLANET.TECH's claim that the same collar that monitors health can also capture enteric methane and convert it to revenue is, if proven, one of the most commercially significant climate technology claims in the ag sector. The economics are real: renewable natural gas from dairy operations in California currently trades at $20–35/MMBtu, and LCFS credits add incremental value.[12]

The key distinction is between point-source methane capture (proven — manure lagoon digesters achieve 80%+ capture) and free-range enteric capture via wearable on a grazing animal (unproven commercially). The company reports a jump from 3% to 34% field capture efficiency in under 5 months — an extraordinary rate of improvement that has not been independently validated or published in peer-reviewed literature.

Moola Risk Factors
  • Technical: Free-range enteric capture at commercial scale is unproven anywhere in the industry
  • Policy: California A.B. 2870 attempted to eliminate avoided-methane credits for animal ag (2024); did not proceed but signals political exposure[13]
  • Market: Voluntary carbon market volatility; LCFS pricing subject to policy reset
  • Off-take: No buyer agreement or off-take contract published
  • Distraction: Dual-product development at pre-revenue stage risks fragmenting the team
If Moola works at scale: PLANET.TECH transitions from a cattle health SaaS to a planetary-scale clean fuel infrastructure platform with a 1.5B-head global addressable herd. The $47B+ Moola TAM is real — the technology just has not been proven.
