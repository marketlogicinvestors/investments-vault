Executive Summary

An option-sized bet on a single binary catalyst

PLANET.TECH is an angel-stage bet on the only cattle health collar claiming to measure pulse oximetry (SpO₂) through bovine hide — a sensor capability no competitor currently offers — distributed via veterinary practices into a US cattle market with $15–20B in annual preventable losses.

01 — Problem

The Problem

An acute catalyst on top of a chronic, multi-billion-dollar loss base

1.1 — Acute Catalyst: New World Screwworm

The New World Screwworm (NWS) outbreak prompted USDA to close the US–Mexico border to live cattle imports in 2025, with 19,677 livestock affected and $1.3B in documented losses in Mexico alone. NWS was detected within 70 miles of the Texas border — directly in PLANET.TECH's beachhead — and remains invisible for the first 48 hours, precisely the detection window the collar claims to close.

1.2 — Chronic Loss Landscape

Across 17 detectable conditions — Bovine Respiratory Disease, mastitis, ketosis, heat stress, lameness and more — annual US cattle losses from preventable health failures total an estimated $15–20B. The farmer's current primary tool is visual inspection at sunrise: a once-daily, reactive observation that misses all early-stage biological signals.

09 — Option

Climate Second Act — Moola

A real macro thesis on an unproven capture mechanism

The Moola platform claims to capture enteric methane from grazing cattle via the same collar — converting it to RNG, carbon credits, or SAF revenue of $400–600/cow/year. The macro thesis is real: livestock supply chains account for ~12–14.5% of global anthropogenic GHG, and US enteric fermentation methane rose >5% from 2010–2020.

Fact-check — 34% field capture: point-source capture (manure-lagoon digesters, 80%+ efficiency) is proven; free-range enteric capture via a wearable on a grazing animal is commercially unproven. The 34% claim is sourced only from a LinkedIn post — no peer review, no published methodology.
Underwrite Moola as a zero-cost call option only. Do not include Moola revenue in base or bear case projections.

17 — Recs

New Business-Model Recommendations

v6.0 additions

Rec 1 — Tiered SKU (Lite / Pro)

$6–7/mo Lite (accelerometer + GPS, ~$20 COGS) for short-cycle feedlot; reserve $10/mo Pro (full pulse-ox) for dairy & cow-calf. Resolves feedlot economics without cannibalizing premium positioning.

Rec 2 — Insurance Data Partnership

Data licensing with Nationwide Agribusiness, Rain & Hail, or USDA RMA for usage-based premiums — a third revenue stream, zero added hardware. A 10–20% premium cut for collared herds bypasses $10/mo price sensitivity entirely.

Rec 3 — Moola + Feed Additive

Position Moola as complementary to Bovaer/Asparagopsis. Combined 50–60% methane reduction becomes a premium compliance product for Walmart & McDonald's Scope 3 commitments — a B2B channel that dwarfs direct-to-farmer.

Rec 4 — Dairy-First Pre-Seed Narrative

Lead the pre-seed pitch with dairy LTV math ($600–840 vs $60–80 feedlot), the CA dairy beachhead, and superior ROI proof density in confined operations. Strongest narrative for institutions and vet-channel development.
