§ 03

Industry & Market

TAM, structure, tailwinds.

Mercury operates in US SMB and startup business banking delivered as a software-led banking platform. The addressable base is ~33M US businesses, with an estimated $300B+ annual revenue pool across net interest income (NII), interchange, and SaaS tooling.6 EST

The 2023 collapse of Silicon Valley Bank durably reshaped startup banking: founders sought a trusted, independent “safe harbor,” accelerating deposit migration toward Mercury and away from concentrated single-bank relationships. This structural tailwind underpins Mercury's deposit growth to ~$20B.6,9

Demand drivers

Higher-for-longer rates lifting NII; founder distrust of legacy banks; rising formation of AI-native startups; multiproduct attach (cards, treasury, AP/AR).6,8

Structure

Fragmented incumbents + well-capitalized fintech challengers. Differentiation increasingly on full-stack ownership (charter) and product breadth, not rate alone.6,14

Cyclicality

NII-heavy model is rate-sensitive; a Fed cutting cycle compresses the largest revenue line — a core risk to monitor.6,8

§ 04

Expansion / NRR

Scale signals

300K+ customers (+50% YoY) and $248B TPV in 2025 (+59% YoY) confirm durable top-of-funnel and usage.5,12,31

§ 06

Legal & Regulatory

Charter, compliance, key risk.

Mercury today operates via a partner-bank model, sponsored by Column N.A. and Choice Financial Group, and pays a revenue share to those partners. In December 2025 Mercury announced its intent to pursue a national bank charter; on 24 April 2026 the OCC granted preliminary conditional approval to establish Mercury Bank, N.A. (OCC Corporate Decision #1372).8,10,13,16

The charter is the central thesis driver: owning the full deposit stack would let Mercury launch direct lending, expand internationally, and eliminate the revenue share paid to partner banks — a structural margin and TAM unlock.6,8,13

Status

Conditional approval secured; full charter is subject to satisfying OCC operating-agreement conditions (capital, risk, compliance build-out) and a defined organization window.13

Primary risk — binary & political

Final charter approval is binary and politically sensitive; delay or denial removes the principal margin-expansion catalyst and is the single largest diligence risk.6,8

§ 07

R&D & Product

Platform, roadmap.

Current platform. A banking operating system spanning checking/savings, debit & corporate cards, treasury/yield, AP/AR and bill pay, plus paid SaaS financial tooling tiered at $35–$350/month.6

Charter-dependent roadmap. With a national charter, the roadmap opens to direct lending products, international expansion, and deeper treasury — converting a distribution platform into a full-stack bank with a structurally wider product surface.6,8
