§ 01 · Executive Summary

The thesis in one paragraph

What you'd say if the partner stopped you in the hallway.

Innerwell is the most credible insurance-accessible ketamine-assisted psychotherapy platform we've found. Five major payers in-network, ~22M covered lives, defensible clinical outcomes (87% report improvement within four weeks), and a structured data layer that could plausibly support an AI clinical decision engine. It is also a company that has not raised institutional capital in nearly four years despite a Greycroft seed lead, employs roughly 41 people, and discloses no revenue. The single most important diligence task is not on this memo. It is a 30-minute call with the CEO to determine whether the funding silence is profitable bootstrapping or quiet stagnation. Until that call happens, every other data point is provisional.

Capital efficiency is a virtue at Series Seed. At month 47 with no follow-on, it becomes a question of whether the team chose not to raise, or could not. The answer determines whether this is a Series A at $8–15M pre or a structured M&A conversation at strategic value.

What we like

First-mover position in insurance-accessible ketamine teletherapy. Clinical outcomes materially differentiated from SSRI baseline. Natural acquisition target for Optum, CVS, Teladoc.

What concerns us

Funding gap signals stalled growth or undisclosed restructure. Founder visibility is thin. DEA Schedule III in-person eval rules remain a structural overhang on at-home ketamine.

Recommended next step

Issue data-room request: ARR, growth, payer mix, CAC/LTV, cap table, regulatory posture. Decision deadline: T+10 business days. No response → drop.

Why it matters

Mental health outcomes are notoriously difficult to commercialize because nobody pays for outcome. They pay for visit. Innerwell's data layer is the foundation of a future value-based contract: if response durability is real, payers will eventually pay for it directly.

The 12-month relapse delta is the metric that matters most for IC. SSRI augmentation cycles are expensive for payers; durable remission is worth real dollars per member per month. Need third-party audit before underwriting this claim.

Discrepancy · Deck vs. External

The pitch deck reports a 65% response rate and 80% lasting improvement. External research (Perplexity / company public-website scrape) reports 87% report improvement within 4 weeks. These are not directly contradictory (different denominators, different time windows, different definitions of "improvement"), but they are not obviously consistent either. The same patient cohort can produce both numbers only under a specific operational definition that should be made explicit.

Diligence ask C2 in the data-room request covers exactly this: the written methodology underlying each cited figure, including denominator, intent-to-treat vs. per-protocol, handling of dropouts, and time-to-measurement window. Until that methodology arrives, treat all three numbers as upper-bound marketing claims, not underwritable outcomes.

§ 04 · Platform & Data Layer

The thing that's actually defensible

Why the data layer matters more than the modality.

Ketamine-assisted psychotherapy is, on its own, a commodity service. Multiple cash-pay competitors offer it; the molecule is generic; the protocol is published. What is not commodity is a longitudinal, structured, payer-aligned outcomes dataset on thousands of mental health patients across modalities (depression, anxiety, treatment-resistant depression) with linked encounter data and adherence signals.

What's in the layer

  • Standardized assessments (PHQ-9, GAD-7) at intake and recurring intervals
  • Encounter notes from psychiatry, therapy, and ketamine sessions
  • Medication adherence and titration history
  • Patient-reported integration outcomes post-ketamine
  • Benchmarked against expected response trajectories

What it could become

  • Clinical decision support: next-best-action surfaced to clinician
  • Risk stratification for non-responders → escalation to ketamine
  • Foundation for value-based contracts with payers
  • Extensible to chronic pain and PTSD (large adjacent TAM)
  • Defensible AI training corpus if FDA/CMS validate response prediction
The dataset is the thesis. If Innerwell has assembled the most depth-rich payer-linked behavioral health outcomes corpus in the U.S., that alone is a multi-hundred-million-dollar strategic asset to Optum, Evernorth, or Anthem's clinical analytics arms, irrespective of underlying revenue.

Discrepancy · Modeled vs Deck

The pitch deck claims a blended LTV/CAC of 2.6x [Deck]. The Perplexity per-channel model implies blended ~7.9x. These cannot both be right under the same definition. Three reconciliations are plausible:

  1. Different LTV horizon. The deck may be reporting first-year LTV only ($800–1,200 range) rather than full-treatment-course LTV. Under this reading, blended LTV ≈ $550 ÷ blended CAC $211 ≈ 2.6x. Plausible. Probably correct.
  2. Different completion-rate assumption. If actual treatment completion is ~25% rather than ~50%, the per-channel LTVs would compress proportionally and the ratio collapses. Possible. Pessimistic case.
  3. Different CAC definition. If the deck includes fully-loaded sales and clinical-acquisition overhead (true total acquisition cost rather than channel-attribution CAC), the denominator could be 2–3x larger. Possible. Most likely combined with reading 1.

Diligence ask A4.b (added). Request the company's CAC and LTV methodology document explicitly. Specifically: (i) the time horizon used for LTV (12-month / 24-month / lifetime), (ii) whether CAC is channel-attributed or fully-loaded, (iii) what completion rate underlies the LTV calculation. Until that arrives, neither the 2.6x nor the 7.9x is underwritable. Both are working hypotheses.

What this section establishes for IC. The unit economics are plausibly very strong on a per-channel basis under reasonable assumptions, especially in the insurance-channel and provider-referral arms where LTV/CAC ratios above 10x are not unusual for in-network behavioral health. The deck claim of 2.6x is consistent with first-year revenue rather than lifetime LTV, which is a fair reporting convention. The strategic question is not whether the unit economics work in any one channel. It is whether the channel mix is actually shifting toward the high-efficiency channels at the rate the strategy requires, or whether D2C / Paid Social remains the volume engine with its much weaker 4.4x economics. That answer is in the trailing 18-month channel-mix waterfall (Diligence Ask A1 and A4).

What the scenarios actually tell us. The spread between Bull and Bear at 2027 is 5.6x ($28M vs $5M). That spread is almost entirely a function of two binary variables: (i) whether the DEA Schedule III in-person eval rules tighten, and (ii) whether the insurance / employer channel actually scales. Neither of those is something the company controls. The IC vote is therefore not really a vote on Innerwell's execution. It is a vote on regulatory direction and payer-channel velocity, with Innerwell as the levered bet. That framing changes the diligence emphasis: less on management and more on regulatory expert calls and payer-side reference checks.

IC implication. A Series A check at $8–15M pre-money pays the Base-case multiple for the Bear-case ARR. That asymmetry is not coincidental. It is precisely what a structured down-round or bridge-style entry looks like, and it is the price band where the deal would clear most easily if the data room confirms the soft signals. Above $15M pre, we are paying Base for Base, which requires conviction the company is currently executing the Base path. Above $22M pre, we are paying for the Bull case and need outcomes-data audit before underwriting.

§ 09 · Risk Heatmap

Where this deal could break

Severity × likelihood, ranked.

Likelihood and severity assessments are pre-diligence judgments based on company-disclosed information and industry base rates; these will be refined post data-room review.

Top three risks · narrative

1. DEA Schedule III rules. The post-COVID telehealth flexibility for Schedule III prescribing remains under active DEA revision. A reversion to strict in-person evaluation requirements would compress Innerwell's at-home ketamine model directly. Compliance posture and contingency plan are essential pre-IC.

2. Funding gap signal. Forty-seven months without disclosed financing in the most active mental health funding environment in a decade is the single loudest signal in this deck. Either the company is profitably bootstrapping (verifiable) or it has run an unsuccessful process (also verifiable). The gap itself is information.

3. D2C CAC competition. BetterHelp, Talkspace, and Cerebral collectively spend hundreds of millions in performance marketing annually. Innerwell cannot win the D2C CAC war and should not try; the strategy must lean into payer-channel acquisition where the larger players are structurally weaker.

Risk-mitigated thesis preconditions

For this deal to clear IC at Series A pre-money $8–15M, three conditions must hold simultaneously:

  • Verified ARR of $3M+ growing at > 10% MoM, with insurance > 60% of revenue mix
  • Demonstrated DEA compliance posture (written SOP, in-person eval workflow, regulatory counsel sign-off)
  • Plausible founder narrative for the funding gap (profitable bootstrap, deliberate slow-build, or current Series A process with named lead)

Failure of any one of the three above → recommend pass.
