§ 02

Company Overview & Products

Two crises, one platform, two flagship devices.

The platform addresses two compounding crises, as framed by the founder: (1) cattle are responsible for 14% of near-term global warming (80% of dairy methane from cow burps); (2) dairy farming is structurally broken — farmers earn roughly 1¢ profit per 11 gallons of milk (profitable only 3 of the last 20 years), and lose an estimated $65B annually to preventable disease and death. The $65B preventable-loss figure is broadly consistent with peer-reviewed estimates of global dairy disease burden[8].

Lead Product

Moola

World’s first wearable methane-capture device for cattle, converting enteric emissions — cow burps — directly into pipeline-quality renewable natural gas (RNG). Emissions become a new recurring revenue stream of $400–600/cow/year in RNG and carbon credits. Has achieved 34% field capture efficiency, up from 3% in 12 weeks, with 98% bench efficiency.

“Whoop for Cows”

Mootality

Standalone health-monitoring collar tracking 17 causes of preventable death, replacing visual observation with continuous sensor-driven early detection. Industry-first pulse oximetry (through hide, no shaving) enables early identification of Bovine Respiratory Disease (BRD), the leading killer of cattle (replacement cost $4,000–$8,000+/animal). Adds GPS, accelerometer, and an AI chatbot with multi-parameter per-cow baselines (Claude Sonnet 4.6).

Underwriting posture: treat Mootality as the base case (a cattle-health SaaS+HaaS business) and Moola as a free call option. Do not model Moola revenue in the base case until capture efficiency is independently validated at scale and an off-take exists.
§ 03

Secret Sauce — Two-Pronged Flywheel

Land with health intel, expand to methane; vets as a force multiplier.
  1. Health-intel wedge. Mootality is the “foot in the door” — it builds trust and gathers data, then farmers upgrade to Moola. Every Mootality farm is a future Moola customer: one acquisition cost, two revenue streams, a compounding data moat.
  2. Vet market. Industry-first pulse oximetry drives vet adoption — one vet adoption equals 50+ farm warm introductions.

Analyst view. The flywheel is the most original element of the thesis, but it is structurally unproven: there are no signed vet contracts yet (per the founder’s own traction data), so the entire low-CAC distribution claim remains a hypothesis to be tested in deep diligence (Gate 3).

§ 10

Bull and Bear, Side by Side

Built from founder claims and independently verified data.
↑ Bull Case
  • Pulse oximetry works in the field; Texas A&M pilot confirms early-detection lead time and real payback. Farmers pay $10/head/mo at scale.
  • Vet channel converts: one adoption = 50+ warm farm intros, building a low-CAC distribution network incumbents can’t replicate quickly.
  • Entry at $6M cap is a steep discount to the verified $2B Halter benchmark[1]; pre-seed at $25M unlocks on milestones.
  • Moola converts the same install base into a methane revenue stream — one CAC, two revenue lines — turning PT into climate infrastructure.
  • 7 patents + compounding per-cow biometric data create a defensible moat strategics (Merck, Zoetis, energy majors) would pay a premium for.
↓ Bear Case
  • Pulse-ox signal is noisy in real conditions; accuracy insufficient for clinical detection. Differentiation collapses to GPS + activity vs. better-funded incumbents.
  • 46 LOIs / $255M are a wishlist; alpha farms don’t convert to paid at expected rates and vets decline to become software distributors.
  • Fully-loaded COGS stays well above the $30–40 target (alpha is $85); margins compress and a hardware working-capital crunch hits before pre-seed.
  • Halter adds biometrics with its $220M war chest and ~1M-collar base, commoditizing PT’s sensor edge[1].
  • Moola stays commercially unviable; carbon/LCFS pricing weakens; the climate story distracts a thin equity-only team and $500K burns faster than planned.

Recommendation & Gates

For IC.
Recommendation

INITIAL DUE DILIGENCE — Proceed to Deep Diligence

PLANET.TECH warrants advancing to deep diligence. The macro tailwind is real and government-confirmed: the New World Screwworm outbreak has reached the US, with the first domestic case in ~60 years confirmed in South Texas and the US–Mexico border closed to livestock since July 2025[6][7]. The category is validated by Halter’s verified $220M / $2B round[1], and the $6M entry cap is attractive relative to that ceiling. The differentiated pulse-ox claim, the vet-channel GTM, and the Moola option are compelling but unproven. Proceed to deep diligence; advance to investment only after clearing the four gates below.

Four Gates to Advance
  • Gate 1 — Sensor data: raw pulse-ox / heart-rate accuracy vs. veterinary reference across breeds, hide colors, and motion; detection sensitivity/specificity for the 17 conditions.
  • Gate 2 — BOM & margin: fully-loaded BOM with supplier quotes supporting the path from $85 alpha COGS to the $30–40 target at volume.
  • Gate 3 — Pipeline & vet proof: named pipeline with contract status, deposits, deployment dates; ≥1 signed vet practice with documented economics.
  • Gate 4 — Moola validation: independent verification of field capture efficiency and a use-of-funds budget with bridge plan to the $25M pre-seed.
Conditions to Track
  • Alpha → paid conversion of the four mid-June contracts
  • Texas A&M P1 pilot protocol and endpoints (Sept 2026)
  • Patent application numbers and claim scope for the 7 filings
  • Confirmed full-time headcount (8 vs. ~14 referenced)
  • Cap-table / prior SAFE obligations before pre-seed conversion
  • Pro-rata right into the $25M pre-seed — key term to negotiate

Proposed Participation

  • Instrument: SAFE at $6M post-money cap (YC Post-Money SAFE)
  • Posture: small option-value check now; real capital deployed at pre-seed after Texas A&M data and paid conversion
  • Key right: pro-rata participation in the $25M pre-seed
  • Timing: deep-diligence sprint to clear four gates; revisit before pre-seed close
