01 — Executive Summary

The thesis in one paragraph

What you'd say if the partner stopped you in the hallway.

Aldabra 4 is a structurally clean, post-IPO SPAC sponsored by a team with the most credible completion record in the category—three prior Terrapin SPACs (Aldabra/GLDD, Aldabra 2/Boise, Terrapin 3/Yatra), all of which closed business combinations across three different market regimes. The vehicle holds approximately $300.15M of public trust at $10.00 per unit, trades at $9.88 (May 15, 2026), and discloses a $500M–$2B EV target framework across six themes. The deck's “100% success” framing, however, conflates deal completion with shareholder return—the most recent precedent (Yatra) currently sits near $1.73 despite operational recovery. The single most important diligence task is a structural read of the S-1, 10-K, and most recent 8-Ks to model trust per share, redemption mechanics, founder promote dilution, and PIPE/forward-purchase availability before any position is sized.

Completion durability is real and rare. But the asymmetric setup at trust value is only as good as the structure underneath it. The deck is sponsor marketing—the SEC filings control.

What we like

Repeat sponsor with operator-led CEO seat (Yanofsky, Dunkin/Panera/Fidelity Ventures). Team continuity across all three prior SPACs. Trading at or near trust preserves an asymmetric downside.

What concerns us

Yatra precedent: operating recovery did not translate into public-equity recovery. Mandate breadth (six themes) makes sourcing edge hard to underwrite. Standard SPAC promote and warrant dilution still applies at de-SPAC.

Recommended next step

Issue structural-diligence pack (EDGAR pull, de-SPAC return ledger on Terrapin precedents, conflicts register, entry-price discipline test). Re-present to IC within 21 calendar days.

03 — Sponsor & Track Record

Completion durability is real, return durability less so

Three prior SPACs, three closes. One excellent, one solid, one publicly disappointing.

The sponsor case is Aldabra 4's strongest single argument. Terrapin's principals have closed business combinations in three different market regimes (2006, 2008, 2016) with the same nucleus of personnel. The completion record is genuinely rare for the category. The unresolved question is whether completion translates into shareholder return in the current de-SPAC environment.

Why it matters

A 3-of-3 close rate across two decades is a real signal. It is not the same as a 3-of-3 win rate for public shareholders. GLDD and Boise are clean wins; Yatra, the most recent and most relevant precedent, is currently a substantial loss for IPO-basis public holders despite operational recovery.

Need an independently computed return ledger before underwriting the headline track record.

Discrepancy: Deck Framing vs. Public-Shareholder Read

The deck reports 100% success rate and headline IRRs of 100.6% / 14.2% on Aldabra 1 and Aldabra 2. It also discloses that Yatra's stock currently sits at $1.73. These are not contradictory (different vehicles, different time windows) but they are not obviously consistent with the “100% success” framing either. Diligence ask D1 covers exactly this—an independently computed public-shareholder return ledger for each prior SPAC.

06 — Target Strategy & Sourcing

Six themes, one EV band

Credible but broad—sponsor edge needs further proof.

The deck states an ideal target enterprise value of $500M to $2B and articulates six investment themes. The S-1 narrows this slightly toward technology-enabled targets. Reconciling deck vs. S-1 framing is a structural-diligence item.

Stated themes (deck p. 8)

  • Fundamental changes in socio-economics and demographics
  • Under-managed IP, proprietary practices, or other intangible assets
  • Competitive advantage / sustainable barriers to entry
  • Fundamental changes in competitive dynamics
  • Technology disrupting markets
  • Large mature PE-backed companies seeking liquidity

How to read it

The framework is consistent with Terrapin's historical sourcing logic (GLDD—Jones Act moat, Boise—subsidiary in out-of-favor industry, Yatra—growth in fast-growing major economy). It is also broad—six themes effectively give the sponsor permission to chase almost anything.

What we want to see in diligence: proprietary access, sourcing infrastructure, or repeatable pattern recognition that converts breadth into edge.

10 — Recommendation

Decision and conditions

Recommendation

Track — Conditional Advance to Structural Diligence

Aldabra 4 warrants a dedicated 21-day structural-diligence sprint, not yet a position. The sponsor-quality and completion-durability case is genuinely strong; the public-shareholder-return case requires the return ledger on prior SPACs before any conviction is earned. We recommend executing the seven P0 diligence items, re-presenting to IC with a structure sheet and return ledger, and only then defining a position size and entry-price discipline.

Conditions to Advance

  • Return ledger shows ≥1 of 3 prior SPACs produced >10% public-shareholder IRR from de-SPAC
  • Trust per share verified at or above $10.00
  • Combination deadline ≥9 months forward at IC date
  • PIPE / forward-purchase posture documented or credibly available
  • Promote dilution under base-case redemption ≤25 percentage points to public Class A
  • Conflict register documented, no undisclosed related-party items

Conditions to Pass

  • Return ledger shows all 3 prior SPACs produced negative public-shareholder IRR from de-SPAC
  • Trust per share below $10.00 due to expense leakage or prior extension
  • Combination deadline <6 months without credible extension mechanic
  • Promote dilution under base-case redemption >35 percentage points to public Class A
  • Undisclosed related-party item discovered in S-K 404 review

Proposed indicative posture: if conditions are met, small position sized at or below trust value, with a defined re-evaluation gate at announce. No position to be opened above $10.05 absent a specific structural reason.
