§ 01

Executive Summary

The thesis in one paragraph — what the market is missing

Energy Transfer is the infrastructure bet on American energy supremacy — the toll-road operator for every molecule of natural gas, NGL, crude oil, and refined product that moves through the most prolific production basins in the United States. At $19.10 per unit, it trades at 8.4x 2026E EBITDA against peers at 9.7–11.2x, yielding 7.2% with 1.72x distribution coverage. The discount reflects a founder-controlled MLP governance structure, a 2020 distribution cut, and a Lake Charles LNG reversal. What the market is not pricing: AI/data center gas demand inflection (three named contracts, ~$440M unmodeled EBITDA), the $13–21B of ET-entity acquisition headroom, and a succession catalyst that could close 1–2 turns of multiple in a single announcement.

What we like
  • Fee-based fortress. 90%+ of segment margin fee-based. Volume is the only sensitivity — not price. Take-or-pay and MVC contracts across gathering, transport, and fractionation.
  • AI demand tailwind — three named contracts. Meta Hyperion (Tiger Pipeline, 250k MMBtu/d, 20-year, Feb 2028). CloudBurst (Oasis Pipeline, 450k MMBtu/d). Oracle (~900 MMcf/d, active). ~$440M of annual EBITDA not in consensus.
  • Desert Southwest capital efficiency. Phase 2 upsized: +0.8 Bcf/d for only $900M additional = 4.2x build multiple vs. 6x headline. Market is modeling the wrong return on ET's best project.
  • Valuation gap. 2-turn discount on $18.4B EBITDA = ~$36.8B of implied market cap gap. At 3.43B units: ~$10.70/unit of trapped value = 56% of current price.
  • Canadian crude hedge. Southern Illinois Connector (100 MBpd, 2028) and DAPL Phase 2 (250 MBpd, 2029+) provide volume floor independent of Permian drilling.
What concerns us
  • The DCF leak. EBITDA grew 3% in 2025. DCF fell $8.4B → $8.2B. Interest expense absorbed the entire operating improvement — up $600M to $4.2B.
  • Governance discount is structural. GP control locks out ~40% of institutional buyer universe permanently until C-Corp conversion. The 2020 Preferred Unit controversy is in institutional memory.
  • Subsidiaries at leverage ceiling. SUN at 4.0x post-Parkland. USAC at 4.0x post-J-W Power. USAC equity is negative (−$112M). Only ET entity has deal capacity.
  • Capex execution risk. Desert Southwest at $5.6B, Q4 2029. ET history includes a $3.3B Rover Pipeline write-down. 18-month slip = $800M–$1.2B 2027 EBITDA miss.
  • Succession unpriced. Marshall McCrea retiring by Dec 31 2026. No successor named. Single biggest unpriced event in ET's near-term trajectory.

§ 08

Critical Assumption Failure Analysis

Where the model breaks first — and what leaders must watch before it shows in the quarterly report

  • Assumption 1 — Capex-to-EBITDA conversion on schedule OK
    Desert Southwest ($5.6B, Q4 2029) and Hugh Brinson (Q4 2026) deliver on time. 18-month slip on either project = $800M–$1.2B 2027 EBITDA miss. Bridge thesis collapses.
    ▶ Signal to watch: FERC permit status for Transwestern Desert Southwest (pre-filing Q1 2026). Quarterly capex spend vs. budget. EPC contractor delay language on earnings calls.
  • Assumption 2 — Interest expense stabilizes as capex cycle peaks OK
    Interest at $4.2B in 2025. If refinancing at higher rates or Fed re-hikes, every $500M of incremental interest = $500M of additional DCF compression. At $4.7B interest, 2026E coverage drops to ~1.62x.
    ▶ Signal to watch: Quarterly interest expense line in ET P&L. Debt maturity schedule and refinancing calendar. Fed funds rate trajectory. Floating-rate exposure disclosure (~20% of $60B = $12B).
  • Assumption 3 — SUN delivers $250M Parkland synergies by 2028 OK
    Parkland closed Oct 31 2025. U.S. Parkland business struggled pre-acquisition. If integration underperforms, SUN EBITDA stays at $2.1B rather than growing to $3.1–3.3B. Removes ~$1B from consolidated guidance.
    ▶ Signal to watch: SUN quarterly EBITDA by segment. U.S. fuel margin per gallon (Q4 2025 benchmark: 17.7¢). Parkland integration update on SUN earnings calls. Any SUN leverage guidance revision.
  • Assumption 4 — Data center contracts convert to contracted volumes OK
    Bramhall: "13 or 14 state conversations." Oracle, Meta Hyperion, CloudBurst are named and contracted — but AI hyperscaler capex compression could slow buildout. At $0.50/MMBtu tariff, named DC EBITDA falls from $437M to ~$291M.
    ▶ Signal to watch: New data center contract announcements. AI hyperscaler capex guidance (Microsoft, Google, Meta quarterly calls). ET disclosing any new "Power & AI Infrastructure" revenue line in segment reporting.
  • Assumption 5 — ET entity leverage stays below 4.5x through any acquisition OK
    ET entity at 3.34x with $21B theoretical headroom. But rating agencies calculate leverage on a consolidated family basis. A large ET-entity acquisition could trigger consolidated leverage review even if ET-entity-only stays below 4.5x.
    ▶ Signal to watch: Rating agency leverage methodology notes. Any credit watch or outlook change (Moody's, S&P, Fitch). ET entity debt issuance announcements. Any "beyond North America" deal announcement structure.
