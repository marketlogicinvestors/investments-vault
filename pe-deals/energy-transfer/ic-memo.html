Executive Summary
What the market is missing. Why now. What we need to believe.

Energy Transfer is the toll-road operator for American energy: 140,000 miles of pipeline, 44 states, every major production basin. At $19.10, it trades at 8.4x 2026E EBITDA of $18.4B against MLP peer Enterprise Products at 10.9x and C-Corp peers at 10.5-11.2x. The discount is real. It reflects governance (founder-controlled GP, 2020 distribution cut, no S&P 500 inclusion) and a credit rating (BBB-) one notch below peers. What the discount does not reflect is the AI demand inflection now showing up in signed contracts, the free cash flow surge arriving in 2027 as the capex cycle peaks, and the succession catalyst that could close 1-2 valuation turns in a single announcement.

ET's model: capital-light

ET supplies firm gas via existing and lateral pipelines. VoltaGrid, Fermi, Nexus own and operate the generation. ET earns transportation tariff without generation capex or merchant power risk. Williams is spending $5.1B building power plants. ET is spending only on laterals and interconnections. ET gets the AI infrastructure revenue without the generation risk.

Real constraint: turbines, not pipelines

Gas supply is not the bottleneck (US produces 104 Bcf/d). ET's mainline network is the moat (140,000 miles, 44 states). The real bottleneck is F-class gas turbine delivery (18-36 month lead times) and ERCOT grid interconnection (3-5 year queue). Both sit with ET's partners, not ET. Take-or-pay protects ET regardless of turbine delivery schedule.

Bull vs Bear
The full return surface in one view.

Bull case: $40-55 unit price by 2028

  • EBITDA executes to $21B+. Desert Southwest on schedule, Hugh Brinson Phase 2 Q1 2027, AI contracts ramp to $700M+ EBITDA.
  • McCrea succession lands well. Named commercial successor with midstream credibility. Market closes 1-2 turns of governance discount in a single announcement.
  • Multiple re-rates from 8.4x to 10x+. Institutional re-entry as governance improves. C-Corp discussion resurfaces. S&P 500 eligibility becomes real.
  • FCF surplus deployed as buybacks. Long uses 2027-2028 FCF surplus at $19-25/unit to reduce share count by 3-8%. Accretive at any price below $35.
  • International JV announced. "Beyond North America" materializes. JV with infrastructure fund or sovereign wealth fund. Off-balance-sheet accretive EBITDA.
  • Credit upgrade to BBB. Reduced leverage + stronger coverage + governance improvement triggers upgrade. Lowers interest cost on $60B+ of debt materially.

Bear case: $17-22 unit price, flat returns

  • AI capex moderates 20%. New contract pipeline dries up. DC EBITDA stays at $440M, doesn't grow to $700M-$1B. Unmodeled upside never shows up.
  • Desert Southwest slips 12 months. FERC certificate delayed. $500M of 2028 EBITDA slides to 2030. Thesis intact but extended by 12 months.
  • Rates rise 150bps. Floating exposure of $12B adds $180M annual interest. Coverage drops to 1.56x. Distribution preservation mode replaces distribution growth mode.
  • Succession disappoints. No named successor by Q3 2026. Governance discount hardens. Multiple stays at 8.4x or compresses further. $37B valuation gap persists.
  • Both AI and rates move against simultaneously. Dual compression scenario. 2028E EBITDA $18.5B not $21.8B. Coverage 1.56x. Unit price flat to down at $17-20.
  • Return profile: 7% carry trade. In the bear scenario, ET does not break. Distribution is safe. Coverage stays above 1.5x kill switch. But total return over 3 years is distribution income only. Capital appreciation: zero.

The Bramhall Conversation
30 minutes. Three observations. One peer-level question. Sequence matters.
Observation 01 — Open with this The DCF Leak
"Your 2025 EBITDA hit a record $15.98B, up 3%. But DCF fell from $8.4B to $8.2B. That gap is interest expense, up $600M to $4.2B, absorbing the entire operating improvement. Q1 2026 shows the inflection: $2.7B DCF vs $2.3B prior year. Is that capex peaking, or refinancing hitting the income statement first?"
WHY THIS WORKS: Opens with a number Bramhall knows intimately. Shows you read the P&L, not just the EBITDA line. The question gives him an opening to discuss the January 2026 $3B refinancing and the interest rate management strategy, which is genuinely interesting to him.
Observation 02 Desert Southwest Capital Efficiency
"The upsize from 1.5 to 2.3 Bcf/d for only $900M additional, that is 4.2x on the incremental capacity, not 6x. The market is modeling the wrong return on your best project. At correct economics, Desert Southwest adds closer to $900M-$1B in steady-state EBITDA, not the $650M consensus is using."
WHY THIS WORKS: Most analysts cite the $5.6B headline and the 2.3 Bcf/d capacity without separating Phase 1 and Phase 2 economics. Showing you disaggregated the phases tells Bramhall you read the June JPMorgan presentation, not just the earnings summary. He will confirm or correct the EBITDA number. Either way you have moved into a technical peer conversation.
Observation 03 The $25B Backlog and Disclosure Opportunity
"The June presentation shows seven named power and data center agreements, 8.3+ GW of behind-the-meter capacity, $25B of contracted firm transportation fee revenue at 18-year weighted average life. Most analysts are still modeling three contracts. Has ET considered presenting the AI/power segment with a backlog-style disclosure, the way LNG terminals do, rather than embedding it in segment EBITDA? At 18-year WAL, this is structurally closer to a regulated utility than a commodity midstream operator."
WHY THIS WORKS: The backlog disclosure question is genuinely new. LNG terminal operators like Cheniere disclose contracted revenue backlogs as a headline metric because it drives their valuation. ET is not doing this. The suggestion that ET should consider it is either something Bramhall has already thought about (in which case you have validated his thinking) or something he hasn't (in which case you have given him something useful). Both outcomes serve you.
Peer-Level Question — Ask Last M&A Architecture
"Your April comments about beyond North America were new language. SUN is at 4.0x ceiling. USAC in deleverage mode. ET direct has $13-21B of theoretical headroom. With Tom Long becoming sole CEO, is the international M&A signal something he has personally committed to, or was that framing more reflective of Mackie's commercial vision? And if it's live, are you thinking JV structure, same architecture as Parkland, to keep it off ET's balance sheet?"
WHY THIS WORKS: This question names the succession dynamic directly without being provocative. It connects the "beyond North America" signal to the leadership change. Bramhall cannot answer it publicly and will not answer it fully privately. But the question tells him you understand the post-McCrea strategic question that he is living with right now. That is the peer signal. You are not asking him to confirm a fact. You are asking him to react to a thesis you have already formed.
Downside Question — Use if tone is right The Meta Signal and Distribution Bottleneck
"Meta offering spare compute externally is the first signal that AI buildout may have gotten ahead of near-term demand absorption. ET's contracts are protected by take-or-pay minimums. But the pipeline of new deals may face tougher MVC negotiations. The real distribution bottleneck in the AI gas story is not pipeline capacity, it's turbine delivery lead times. ET's capital-light model puts that risk with VoltaGrid and Fermi. Is that structural risk separation explicit in how ET frames the model to institutional investors?"
WHY THIS WORKS: Shows you have read current news (Meta signal is two weeks old). Frames the risk specifically. Acknowledges ET's protection (take-or-pay). Asks about investor communication strategy, not about whether the risk is real. Bramhall will either say it's already in the investor narrative (which tells you he's thought about it) or he'll be interested in how you framed it (which tells you it isn't yet).
